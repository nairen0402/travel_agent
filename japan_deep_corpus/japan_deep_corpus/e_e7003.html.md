Hikone Station lies along the JR Tokaido Main Line (also locally referred to as Biwako Line) just one station (4 minutes, 190 yen, frequent departures) southwest of Maibara Station, a station served by the [JR Tokaido Shinkansen](e2018_tokaido.html), which connects [Tokyo](e2164.html) with [Kyoto](e2158.html) and [Osaka](e2157.html).

Covered by: [Japan Rail Pass](/e/e2361.html)

Maibara can be reached from [Tokyo](e2164.html) by direct [Hikari](e2018_tokaido.html) trains on the [Tokaido Shinkansen](e2018_tokaido.html) in a little over two hours. The one way journey costs around 12,000 yen and is fully covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html).

Covered by: [Japan Rail Pass](/e/e2361.html)

Direct JR "special rapid" trains to Hikone take 50 minutes and cost 1170 yen from [Kyoto](e2158.html) or 90 minutes and 1980 yen from [Osaka](e2157.html). By [shinkansen](e2018.html) via Maibara, the trip takes 30 minutes from Kyoto (2350 yen for unreserved seats) or 45 minutes from Osaka (4700 yen).

Above fees and schedules are subject to change. Be sure to check current yen [exchange rates](e2196.html).

The [castle](e7001.html) can be reached from Hikone Station in a 15-20 minute walk along the main road. Most of the city's other attractions are located within walking distance of the castle. Alternatively, [taxis](e2021.html) are readily available in front of the station.
