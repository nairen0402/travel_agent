### Transportation

* 

  [Railway Museum••](e6527.html)

  Tetsudo Hakubutsukan Station, New Shuttle  
  Hours: 10:00 to 17:00 (entry until 16:30)  
  Closed: Most Tuesdays and [New Year](e2276.html) holidays  
  Admission: 1600 yen

  Located in [Saitama](e6525.html) north of Tokyo, the [Railway Museum](e6527.html) is a rail enthusiast's dream. It features a large number of full-size trains on display, train conducting simulators of actual Japan Railway lines and a large outdoor circuit with drivable mini trains.
* 

  Subway Museum

  Underneath Kasai Subway Station  
  Hours: 10:00 to 17:00 (entry until 16:30)  
  Closed: Mondays (or following day if Mon is a  [holiday](e2062.html)), Dec 30 to Jan 3  
  Admission: 220 yen

  The Subway Museum is devoted to Tokyo's subways. Like the Railway Museum, but much smaller in scale, it features full-size train exhibits, train conducting simulators and model train displays.

### Commerce

* 

  Currency Museum•

  Short walk from [Mitsukoshi-mae Subway Station](e3033.html)  
  Hours: 9:30 to 16:30 (entry until 16:00)  
  Closed: Mondays (except [national holidays](e2062.html)), December 29 to January 4  
  Admission: Free

  Maintained by the Bank of Japan and located across the street from the Bank of Japan's main building, the Currency Museum displays the history of money in Japan and around the world. English explanations and pamphlets are available.
* 

  The Seiko Museum Ginza•

  Short walk from [Ginza Subway Station](e3005.html)  
  Hours: 10:30 to 18:00, [advance reservations](https://museum.seiko.co.jp/en/) are required  
  Closed: Mondays, [New Year](e2276.html) holidays  
  Admission: Free

  This excellent museum in the [Ginza](e3005.html) district primarily educates its visitors about the history of Seiko, Japan's most prominent watch manufacturer, but also has interesting displays and explanations about the history of clocks in general. A lot of English information is provided. Advance reservations are necessary to visit the museum.
* 

  Yebisu Brewery Tokyo

  7 minute walk from [Ebisu Station](e3028.html)  
  Hours: 12:00 to 20:00 (weekdays), 11:00 to 19:00 (weekends)  
  Closed: Tuesdays (or next day if Tuesday is a [holiday](e2062.html)), [New Year](e2276.html) holidays  
  Admission: Free

  Located at [Yebisu Garden Place](e3028.html) near the Photography Museum, the Yebisu Brewery Tokyo is a small brewery and museum about the history and techniques of making beer. There is also a tap room where exclusive brews can be enjoyed for a fee and a gift shop.
* 

  Postal Museum Japan

  Inside [Tokyo Skytree Town](e3064_town.html)  
  Hours: 10:00 to 17:30  
  Closed: No closing days  
  Admission: 300 yen

  The Postal Museum is located on the 9th floor of [Solamachi East](e3064_solamachi.html). It has a wide collection of stamps from all over the world, as well as interactive displays and exhibits showing the history of the postal services in Japan.
* 

  Tobacco and Salt Museum

  10 minute walk south of [Tokyo Skytree](e3064.html)  
  Hours: 10:00 to 17:00 (entry until 16:30)  
  Closed: Mondays (or Tuesday if Monday is a [holiday](e2062.html)), [New Year](e2276.html) holidays  
  Admission: 100 yen

  This museum introduces the history of tobacco and salt in Japan and the world. It is operated by Japan Tobacco (JT) which used to monopolize the production and sale of tobacco and salt.
