* 

  [Day 1 - Tokyo to Yaeyama](e2430.html)

  Fly from [Tokyo](e2164.html) to the [Yaeyama Islands](e7200.html) in southern [Okinawa](/list/e1247.html). Stay for three nights.
* 

  [Day 2 and 3 - Yaeyama Islands](e7200.html)

  Spend two more days exploring the Yaeyama Islands, including [Ishigaki Island](e7201.html), [Iriomote Island](e7225.html) and [Taketomi Island](e7250.html).
* 

  [Day 4 - Yaeyama to Kagoshima](e4600.html)

  Leave the [Yaeyama Islands](e7200.html) for [Kagoshima](e4600.html) and spend two nights there.
* 

  [Day 5 - Kagoshima](e4601.html)

  Explore the city of [Kagoshima](e4600.html) and check out [Sakurajima](e4601.html), an active [volcano](e2327.html) and the symbol of Kagoshima.
* 

  [Day 6 - Kagoshima to Kurokawa Onsen via Aso](e4552.html)

  Visit [Mount Aso](e4552.html) along your way from Kagoshima to [Kurokawa Onsen](e4575.html). Stay at a [ryokan](e2029.html) in Kurokawa.
* 

  [Day 7 - Kurokawa Onsen to Fukuoka](e2161.html)

  Leave for [Fukuoka](e2161.html) and spend the night there. Explore the city and check out the city's famous [food stalls](e4803.html) (yatai) in the evening.
* 

  [Day 8 - Fukuoka to Hiroshima](e3401.html)

  Head to [Hiroshima](e2160.html) and check out the city and nearby [Miyajima Island](e3401.html). Spend the night on Miyajima if the budget allows.
* 

  [Day 9 - Hiroshima to Osaka via Himeji](e3501.html)

  Visit [Himeji Castle](e3501.html) along the way from Hiroshima to [Osaka](e2157.html). Overnight in Osaka.
* 

  [Day 10 - Osaka to Koyasan](e4900.html)

  Travel to [Koyasan](e4900.html), the center of [Shingon Buddhism](e2055.html). Stay overnight at a [temple (shukubo)](e4905.html) to experience a monk's lifestyle and eat vegetarian monk's cuisine.
* 

  [Day 11 to 13 - Kyoto](e2158.html)

  Move on to [Kyoto](e2158.html) and spend three days in the old capital. Consider our suggested [Kyoto itineraries](e3950.html).
* 

  [Day 14 - Day trip to Nara](e2165.html)

  In a side trip from Kyoto, visit [Nara](e2165.html), home of Japan's first permanent capital. Visit some of the oldest and largest [temples](e2058.html) in Japan and feed the deer that roam freely in the city.
* 

  [Day 15 - Kyoto to Kanazawa](e2167.html)

  Head to [Kanazawa](e2167.html) and explore this interesting town. [Kenrokuen](e4200.html) is considered Japan's best [landscape garden](e2099.html) by many.
* 

  [Day 16 - Kanazawa to Shirakawago](e5950.html)

  Travel to [Shirakawago](e5950.html), see the nice village and stay [overnight at a gassho-zukuri farmhouse](e5952.html).
* 

  [Day 17 - Shirakawago to Takayama](e5903.html)

  Head to the beautifully preserved [old town](e5903.html) of [Takayama](e5900.html) in [Gifu Prefecture](/list/e1220.html).
* 

  [Day 18 - Takayama to Kamikochi](e6040.html)

  Proceed to [Kamikochi](e6040.html), a popular mountain resort in the [Northern Japan Alps](e6043.html). Do some [hiking](e2427_where.html) and overnight there. Note that Kamikochi is not accessible in winter.
* 

  [Day 19 - Kamikochi to Yamanouchi via Matsumoto](e6025.html)

  On your way from Kamikochi to [Yamanouchi](e6025.html), stop by [Matsumoto](e6050.html) to check out [Matsumoto Castle](e6051.html), one of the most complete and beautiful among Japan's original [castles](e2296.html). Overnight in [Yamanouchi](e6025.html), the home of the [snow monkeys](e6028.html).
* 

  [Day 20 - Yamanouchi to Kusatsu](e7400.html)

  Travel through the mountains to [Kusatsu Onsen](e7400.html) in [Gunma Prefecture](/list/e1209.html), one of Japan's [best hot spring resorts](e2292_where.html). Overnight in Kusatsu. Note that shortest route between Yamanouchi and Kusatsu is closed during the winter.
* 

  [Day 21 to 23 - Kusatsu to Tokyo](e2164.html)

  Proceed to [Tokyo](e2164.html) and spend three nights in the capital. Consider our suggested [Tokyo itineraries](e3051.html).
* 

  [Day 24 - Tokyo to Nyuto Onsen](e3650.html)

  Head north to [Nyuto Onsen](e3650.html), a [hot spring](e2292.html) resort in the mountains of [Akita Prefecture](/list/e1203.html). Overnight at one of the rustic onsen [ryokan](e2029.html).
* 

  [Day 25 - Nyuto Onsen to Hirosaki via Hachimantai](e3701.html)

  Travel through the [Hachimantai Mountains](e3651.html) to [Hirosaki](e3701.html) whose [castle](e3700.html) is one of the country's best [cherry blossom spots](e2011_where.html). Note that the direct route through the Hachimantai Mountains is closed during winter.
* 

  [Day 26 and 27 - Sapporo](e2163.html)

  Take trains from Hirosaki to [Sapporo](e2163.html) and stay two nights in [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html)'s capital. Explore the city and consider a side trip to [Otaru](e6700.html).
* 

  [Day 28 and 29 - Shiretoko](e6850.html)

  Travel across [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html) to the [Shiretoko Peninsula](e6850.html), one of Japan's most beautiful and unspoiled [national parks](e2325.html). Stay for two nights on the peninsula and explore the outdoors.
* 

  [Day 30 - Shiretoko to Tokyo](e2430.html)

  Congratulations on completing the Great Traverse from [Okinawa](/list/e1247.html) to [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html) in 30 days. Fly back from Memanbetsu Airport to [Tokyo](e2164.html) and start on your next adventure!

The above itinerary is somewhat fast-paced. Tourists preferring a slow pace of travel should consider spending more time at some of the destinations along the way.
