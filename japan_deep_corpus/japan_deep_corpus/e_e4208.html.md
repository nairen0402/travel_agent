[Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Kanazawa](e2167.html) are directly connected with each other by the [JR Hokuriku Shinkansen](e2018_nagano.html). The one way ride takes 2.5 to 3 hours, costs around 14,000 yen and is fully covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html) and [Hokuriku Arch Pass](e2361_arch.html). Note that the latter will almost pay off for a round trip between Tokyo and Kanazawa.

The one way trip between [Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Kanazawa](e2167.html) by [highway bus](e2366.html) takes about 7-8 hours and costs around 6000 to 8000 yen depending on the bus and date of travel. There are daytime and overnight buses. Discount bus operators, such as [Willer Express](https://willer-travel.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/ishikawa/kanazawa/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184), offer one way fares from around 4500 yen. Online reservations can be made through [Willer](https://willer-travel.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/ishikawa/kanazawa/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184) and [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en/CourseSearch/11300670001?AreaCd=313&afcd=MDI=).

Kanazawa's [airport](e2365.html) is located in the city of Komatsu, a 40 minute bus ride (1300 yen) from central Kanazawa. [JAL and ANA](e2365.html) operate several flights per day between Tokyo's [Haneda Airport](e2430.html) and [Komatsu Airport](e4282.html). Flight time is one hour. The regular one way fare from Tokyo to Kanazawa is about 30,000 yen, however, [discount tickets](e2364.html) for around 10,000 to 13,500 yen are usually available.

[Kanazawa](e2167.html)'s city center is defined by [Kanazawa Castle Park](e4201.html). All of the city's main attractions are located within a two kilometer radius of the castle park, making it possible for good walkers to explore most of the city on foot. [Buses](e2015.html) and [taxis](e2021.html) are also readily available.

Kanazawa Station is not located centrally, about two kilometers or a ten minute bus ride northwest of the city center. A dense network of [buses](e2015.html), operated by the local Hokutetsu train and bus company, connects the station with the city center and all of Kanazawa's main tourist attractions. Regular city buses cost 210 yen per ride in central Kanazawa.

A useful bus for tourists is the **Kanazawa Loop Bus**, which connects Kanazawa Station with most sights of interests. There is a clockwise (Right Loop) and counter-clockwise (Left Loop) bus, covering slightly different routes They each operate every 15 minutes and cost 210 yen per ride. A 1-day bus pass for 800 yen allows for unlimited use of the loop buses as well as most other local buses in central Kanazawa. In addition, the day pass also provides discounted admission to several designated local tourist attractions. It can be purchased at the Hokutetsu ticket offices on both sides of Kanazawa Station.

Furthermore, there are some **JR buses**, which connect Kanazawa Station with [Kenrokuen](e4200.html) and the [Higashi Chaya District](e4206.html). The buses are covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html), [JR Kansai Hokuriku Area Pass](e2361_kansai_hokuriku.html) and [JR Hokuriku Area Pass](e2361_hokuriku.html), as well as by the above-mentioned 1-day bus pass. There are 1-3 buses per hour, and the ride costs 210 yen without a rail pass.

The **Machi Bus** is another bus line for tourists and shoppers, which connects Kanazawa Station via the city center with [Kenrokuen](e4200.html) every 20 minutes; however, it operates only on weekends and [national holidays](e2062.html). The fare is 100 yen per ride, and it is not covered by the above-mentioned 1-day bus pass.

[IC cards](e2359_003.html), including Suica, Pasmo and Icoca, can be used on the Kanazawa Loop Bus, JR buses and the Machi Bus, but they cannot be used on other city buses.
