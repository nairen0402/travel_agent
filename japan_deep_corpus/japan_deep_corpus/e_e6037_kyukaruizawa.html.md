The following is a suggested one day itinerary of the [Kyu-Karuizawa](e6030.html) district in [Karuizawa](e6030.html). This average paced itinerary makes use of public buses to access the attractions over the course of the day, making it suitable for those who would prefer to not walk too much. Those interested in shopping can consider ending their day at the [Karuizawa Prince Shopping Plaza](e6032.html#prince) or staying overnight to extend their stay and visiting the nearby [Naka-Karuizawa](e6033.html) the following day.

Start

Start at **Karuizawa Station**

**Bus**

25 minutes

Shiraito Waterfall

A waterfall in the forest
30-45 minutes

[Shiraito Waterfall](e6034.html) is a waterfall located in a forested area north of downtown [Karuizawa](e6030.html). The waterfall is three meters high and 70 meters wide and has many streams of water that resemble a curtain of strings of white thread. Shiraito no taki is most beautiful in summer when it is surrounded by lush greenery.

**Bus**

20 minutes

Kyu-Karuizawa Ginza

Shopping street
45-120 minutes

[Kyu-Karuizawa Ginza](e6032.html#ginza) is the area's main shopping street that is lined with many shops offering local products and souvenirs. There are also a variety of dining options at the eateries and restaurants nearby.

**Bus**

20 minutes

Usui Pass Observation Platform

Observation platform
30-45 minutes

The [Usui Pass Observation Platform](e6035.html) offers panoramic views of the nearby mountain ranges, as well as a slightly obscured view of Mt Asama, an active volcano. The platform is also one of the few places in Japan to straddle prefectural borders and allows visitors to be in two prefectures-[Nagano](/list/e1216.html) and [Gunma](/list/e1209.html)-at the same time.

**Bus**

20 minutes

**Bus**

7 minutes

Finish

Karuizawa Station

* 

  [Karuizawa Prince Shopping Plaza](e6032.html#prince)

  Located just across from Karuizawa Station, this is one of Japan's largest outlet malls offering a wide variety of brands, from high fashion to outdoor goods, home and lifestyle products. There are also a number of dining establishments here, making it a one stop tourist attraction. The mall is also surrounded by pleasant greenery and also offers a view of the nearby active volcano, Mount Asama.
* 

  [Kumobaike Pond](e6032.html#kumoba)

  Kumobaike is a photogenic pond located about a 15 minute walk from either the Kyu-Karuizawa Ginza shopping street or Karuizawa Station. The pond is lovely to visit any time of the year but is most popular during the [autumn color](e2014.html) season when leaves around the pond change colors.
