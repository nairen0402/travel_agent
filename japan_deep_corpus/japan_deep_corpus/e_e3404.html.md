[Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Hiroshima](e2160.html) are connected with each other by the [JR Tokaido/Sanyo Shinkansen](e2018_sanyo.html). Direct Nozomi trains require about four hours to reach Hiroshima from Tokyo. By Hikari and Sakura trains, the trip takes about five hours with a transfer of trains at Shin-Osaka Station.

The regular one way fare from Tokyo to Hiroshima is 18,380 yen for a non-reserved seat on any train, around 19,000 yen for a reserved seat on Hikari and Sakura trains and around 19,500 yen for a reserved seat on a Nozomi train. The [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html) is valid on Hikari and Sakura trains, but a supplement fee of 6,500 yen has to be paid if using a Nozomi train.

JAL and ANA operate several [flights](e2365.html) per day between [Hiroshima](e2160.html) and [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s [Haneda Airport](e2430.html), while the low-cost carrier Spring Japan serves Tokyo's [Narita Airport](e2027.html). The regular one way fare by JAL and ANA is around 41,000 yen, but [discounts tickets](e2364.html) can bring the cost down to between 12,000 to 17,000 yen, while fares by Spring Japan start around 6,000 yen. Flight duration is about 90 minutes. Hiroshima Airport is a 50 minute, 1450 yen bus ride outside of the city center.

[Hiroshima](e2160.html) has Japan's largest **tram network** with eight tram lines connecting Hiroshima Station with most of the city's attractions. Tram line number 2 goes as far as Miyajima-guchi, from where ferries depart to [Miyajima](e3401.html). The fare for a single tram ride is a flat 240 yen. The [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html) is not valid on the trams.

The trams enter Hiroshima Station on its second floor, allowing for convenient transfers to/from trains

Major [IC cards](e2359_003.html), including Suica and Icoca, can be used to pay the fare on trams and buses in Hiroshima and on the ferries to [Miyajima](e3454.html) (both companies).

Alternatively, a **one-day pass** for unlimited use of all trams on one calendar day is available for 700 yen. For an additional 300 yen, you get a one-day pass that also includes the ferry ride to Miyajima by Matsudai ferry (but not by JR ferry) and back (including the 100 yen visitor tax). Holders of these one-day passes, furthermore, qualify for a discount on the [Miyajima Ropeway](e3451.html) (1500 yen instead of 2000 yen). Note that the various passes are available as [digital tickets](https://www.mobiry.jp/en/).

The JR-operated **Hiroshima Sightseeing Loop Buses**, called Meipuru-pu (combination of Maple and Loop), serve all of central Hiroshima's major sightseeing attractions (see map above). There are three routes: the orange, green and lemon routes. The lemon route is the most frequent with 3-4 departures per hour, while the other two routes operate once per hour. The buses are covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html) and several regional [JR passes](e2357.html). Without a rail pass, they cost 240 yen per ride or 600 yen for a 1-day pass.

Good walkers will also be able to cover the roughly three kilometers from Hiroshima Station via [Shukkeien](e3403.html) and [Hiroshima Castle](e3402.html) to the [Peace Memorial Park](e3400.html) on foot.
