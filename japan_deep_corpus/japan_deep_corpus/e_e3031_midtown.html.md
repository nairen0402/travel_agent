Tokyo Midtown (東京ミッドタウン) is a [city within the city](e3027.html) in the center of [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s [Roppongi](e3031.html) district. Opened in 2007, the complex includes apartments, offices, shops, restaurants, museums, park space and a luxury hotel. In its center stands one of Tokyo's tallest buildings, the 248 meter tall [Midtown Tower](#tower).

The top floors of the Midtown Tower are occupied by the [Ritz Carlton Tokyo](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/the-ritz-carlton-tokyo.html?aid=371183), one of [Tokyo's best hotels](/hotels/tokyo.html). Tourists who want to enjoy the view from the building can either stay overnight at the hotel (room rates range from 40,000 to 2,000,000 yen per night) or visit one of its high-end restaurants.

The [Galleria](#galleria) building is an elegant 4-story shopping and dining complex that also houses the [Suntory Museum of Art](#suntory), one of the museums of the "Art Triangle Roppongi". Green space is found behind the buildings of Tokyo Midtown, including a pleasant [Japanese-style garden](e2099.html) named [Hinokicho Park](#hinokicho).

* 

  Midtown Tower

  At 248 meters, Midtown Tower used to be [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s tallest building from 2007 to 2014. While office space occupies most of the building, the top floors make up the world class [Ritz-Carlton Tokyo](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/the-ritz-carlton-tokyo.html?aid=371183) hotel and the lower floors contain conference space, a medical center and shops. The restaurants and bars of the Ritz Carlton are also open to non-staying guests.
* 

  Suntory Museum of Art

  Hours: 10:00 to 18:00 (until 20:00 on Fridays and Saturdays)  
  Closed: Tuesdays, [New Year](e2276.html) holidays, between exhibitions  
  Admission: varies by exhibition

  The Suntory Museum of Art shows five or six exhibitions per year, which usually focus on a particular style or theme of traditional Japanese art. Bringing the joy of art to regular life has been one of the museum's guiding principles since its foundation. First opened in 1961, the museum was relocated here in 2007.
* 

  Galleria

  Shops: typically 11:00 to 20:00  
  Restaurants: typically 11:00 to 23:00

  Galleria is Tokyo Midtown's main shopping area with a large number of attractive high-end shops. The complex is attractively designed with wooden hues, soft lighting and a spacious central walkway that extends to the top of the four story structure. Visitors can find fashion, interior design, cafes, restaurants and a [supermarket](e2073.html).
* 

  Hinokicho Park

  Hinokicho Park is a [Japanese-style garden](e2099.html) that belongs to the city and is located on the former site of a feudal lord's villa. Next to it, the Midtown Garden offers even more green space and has a number of cherry trees that flower during the [cherry blossom season](e2011.html) in late March and early April. Tokyo Midtown's various gardens make it one of the greenest city redevelopments in the capital.
* 

  21\_21 Design Sight

  Hours: 10:00 to 19:00 (entry until 18:30)  
  Closed: Tuesdays, [New Year](e2276.html) holidays, between exhibitions  
  Admission: 1400 yen

  21\_21 Design Sight is a museum in the garden of Tokyo Midtown, designed by [star-architect](e2111_architects.html) Ando Tadao. Located mostly underground, the museum's exhibition rooms provide an interesting venue for various, temporary design-related exhibitions.

Tokyo Midtown is directly connected with Roppongi Station on the Oedo Subway Line. The platforms of the Hibiya Subway Line are a 5-10 minute walk away.

### From Tokyo Station

15 minutes, 180 yen by Marunouchi and Hibiya subway lines via Kasumigaseki Station.

### From Shinjuku Station

10 minutes, 220 yen by direct Oedo Subway Line.
