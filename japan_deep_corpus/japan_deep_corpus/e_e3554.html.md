[Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Kobe](e2159.html) are connected with each other by the JR [Tokaido/Sanyo Shinkansen](e2018.html). Nozomi trains require about 160 minutes to reach Shin-Kobe Station from [Tokyo](e2164.html), while direct Hikari trains require roughly 195 minutes. The regular one way fare from Tokyo to Shin-Kobe is 14,420 yen for a non-reserved seat or around 15,000 yen for a reserved seat. The [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html) is valid on Hikari trains, but a supplement needs to be paid on Nozomi trains.

The one way trip from Tokyo to Kobe by [overnight bus](e2366.html) takes about 8-9 hours. The competition between bus companies on the Tokyo - Osaka - Kobe route is fierce and has produced various [discount offers](e2366.html). Fares by discount bus operators, such as [Willer Express](https://willerexpress.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/hyogo/kobe/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184), start from around 5000 yen. Buses on this price level are usually standard buses, which offer little comfort. The one way trip on more comfortable buses costs around 8500 yen. Online Reservations can be made through [Willer](https://willer-travel.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/hyogo/kobe/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184) and [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en/CourseSearch/11700010073?afcd=MDI=&RosenNm=tokyo-kobe).

Multiple flights are operated each day between [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s [Haneda Airport](e2430.html) and [Kobe Airport](e2432.html) by ANA and Skymark Airlines. The regular one way fare by ANA is about 31,000 yen, while [discount tickets](e2364.html) are typically available for around 14,000 yen. The regular one way fare by Skymark Airlines is around 18,000 yen, while discount fares are usually available for below 10,000 yen.

[Kobe](e2159.html)'s busiest railway station is not Kobe Station but **Sannomiya Station** at the city center. Sannomiya Station is served by multiple [railway](e2019.html) companies, including Japan Railways (JR), Hankyu Railway and Hanshin Railway. The [shinkansen](e2018.html) (bullet train) stops at **Shin-Kobe Station**, a short subway ride north of Sannomiya Station.

Distances between the attractions in central [Kobe](e2159.html) are relatively short, and good walkers can cover many of them on foot. There is also a tourist-oriented loop bus, which connects sites such as [Kitano-cho](e3550.html), [Nankinmachi](e3551.html), [Meriken Park](e3552.html) and [Harborland](e3553.html) with Shin-Kobe and Sannomiya Stations. The bus costs 300 yen per ride, while a day pass is available for 800 yen.

[Kobe Airport](e2432.html) is located on a man-made island just off the coast of central Kobe and can be reached by the Port Liner from Sannomiya Station in less than 20 minutes for 340 yen.

### Special Tickets

* **Kobe Meguri 1 Day Coupon** (1000 yen)
    
  Unlimited use of subways, non-JR trains and some buses around Kobe on one calendar day plus a 800 yen coupon to be used on admission to selected tourist sites. Available at non-JR train stations. There are also extended versions of this ticket that include access to Kobe, e.g. the Hanshin version for 1650 yen that includes unlimited use of Hanshin trains from [Osaka](e2157.html) or the Hankyu version for 1800 yen which can be used to access Kobe from as far as [Kyoto](e2158.html) by Hankyu trains.
* **City Loop - Day Pass** (800 yen)
    
  Unlimited rides on the Kobe City Loop bus on one calendar day. A 2-day pass is available for 1200 yen.
* **Subway and Bus Day Pass** (1040 yen)
    
  Unlimited usage of Kobe's two subway lines and Kobe city buses (not including the City Loop) on one calendar day.
