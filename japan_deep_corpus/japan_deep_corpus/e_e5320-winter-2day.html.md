While the [Sapporo](e2163.html) area has enough winter attractions to easily fill an itinerary of several days or more, for those short on time, this moderately-paced itinerary offers a taste of some of the city's best wintertime highlights.

This itinerary focuses on Sapporo's biggest winter event, the [Sapporo Snow Festival](e5311.html), as well as a day of [winter sports](e2262.html) at a ski resort, but depending on your timing and preferences, these can be swapped out with other options like a trip to the nearby port town of [Otaru](e6700.html), [Noboribetsu](e6750.html)'s hot springs or more time exploring Sapporo's [other attractions](e2163.html#section_spot_list).

### Day 1: Sapporo Snow Festival and Downtown

Start

Start at **New Chitose Airport**

Take the **JR Chitose Line** into Sapporo

35 minutes

**Sapporo Station**

Walk to **Odori Park**

10 minutes

Sapporo Snow Festival

World famous winter festival
2-3 hours

The [Sapporo Snow Festival](e5311.html) is Sapporo's most well-known attraction, drawing over two million visitors from around the world each year in early to mid February. The monolithic snow and ice sculptures on display around [Odori Park](e5301.html) are the festival's main attraction, but local cuisine, live shows and winter sports exhibitions keep the energy high from morning till night throughout the weeklong event.

Walk to **Susukino**

15 minutes

Ramen Yokocho

Atmospheric ramen shop-lined alley in Susukino
45 minutes

A great place for a hot lunch on a cold winter day, the Susukino neighborhood's Ramen Yokocho is a small alley abuzz with visitors waiting to buy a bowl of the city's famous Sapporo style [ramen](e2042.html).

Walk back to **Odori Park**

15 minutes

Sapporo TV Tower

Tower with a great view over Odori Park
45 minutes

While enjoying the snow festival, the observation deck at the top of the Sapporo TV Tower, located at the east end of Odori Park, offers a great vantage point over the festival festivitiesâ€”especially at night.

Take the Loop 88 Factory Line to the **Sapporo Beer Museum**

20 minutes

Sapporo Beer Museum and Beer Garden

1-3 hours

The [Sapporo Beer Museum](e5300.html) is a popular destination for both beer and history fans which showcases the beverage's history in Japan and offers tastings at the end of the tour. Next to the museum, the Sapporo Beer Garden offers its famed "Genghis Khan" all-you-can-eat/drink mutton and beer dining experience.

Finish

### Day 2: Winter Sports and Onsen Resorts

Start

Start in downtown Sapporo

Take a bus to **Sapporo Kokusai Ski Resort**

90 minutes

Sapporo Kokusai Ski Resort

Ski Resort with great snow near Sapporo City
4-7 hours

Despite its small size (with only 5 lifts and 7 courses), [Sapporo Kokusai](e5319.html) is known for receiving huge volumes of powder snow, and is one of Hokkaido's most popular resorts thanks to its close proximity to the city.

Take a bus to **Jozankei Onsen**

30 minutes

Hot Springs at Jozankei Onsen

Developed hot spring town
90 minutes or overnight stay

By Japan standards, [Jozankei](e6745.html)'s hot springs are relatively new (discovered in just 1866), but the small town has since bubbled into an mini onsen resort town whose hot baths offer a relaxing finish to a day at the nearby slopes.

Finish

* 

  [Otaru Snow Light Path Festival](e6706.html)

  The harbor town of [Otaru](e6700.html) is a fantastic winter destination near Sapporo (about 30 minutes by train) with a beautifully preserved canal area. In winter, the canal and nearby areas are is lit up at night by hundreds of glowing lanterns at night during the annual [Otaru Snow Light Path Festival](e6706.html).
* 

  [Niseko](e6720.html)

  For skiers or snowboarders looking for a taste of some of Hokkaido's top-tier slopes, the resorts of [Niseko](e6720.html) are considered by many the best in Japan, legendary for their massive amounts of high quality powder snow. The resort area is about two hours from Sapporo by train.
