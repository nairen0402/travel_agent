[Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Kakunodate](e3601.html) are connected by hourly komachi trains along the [JR Akita Shinkansen](e2018_tohoku.html). The one way trip takes around 3 hours, costs about 17,000 yen and is fully covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html), [JR East Tohoku Area Pass](e2361_tohoku.html) and [JR East South Hokkaido Pass](e2361_southhokkaido.html). Seat reservations are mandatory for the Akita Shinkansen.

JAL and ANA operate multiple [flights](e2365.html) per day between [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s [Haneda Airport](e2430.html) and Akita Airport. Flight duration is one hour. The regular one way fare is about 28,000 yen, however, [discount tickets](e2364.html) are typically available for between 12,000 and 17,000 yen. Access to Akita Airport is explained in the [getting around](#section_orientation) section.

[Kakunodate](e3601.html) is best explored on foot. Kakunodate Station is located to the southeast of the [samurai district](e3602.html). It is about a 15 minute walk from the station to the beginning of the [samurai district](e3602.html), and a further 10 minute walk to the district's other end. Walking from the station will take tourists through part of the [merchant district](e3605.html). [Taxis](e2021.html) and rickshaws are also available.

Transportation between Akita Airport and [Kakunodate](e3601.html) is somewhat limited, and as such, [rental car](e2024.html) outlets at the airport offer a convenient option. Direct public transportation between the airport and [Kakunodate](e3601.html) is provided by the Akita Airport Liner. This "taxi" service must be reserved by the day before travel and costs 4500 yen per person. Note that it will not operate for just one passenger. Alternatively, limousine buses takes passengers as far as Akita Station for 950 yen, where they can then board a train for [Kakunodate](e3601.html).
