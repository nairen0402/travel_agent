Below is a list of [Nagasaki](e2162.html)'s most popular spots for cherry blossom viewing ([hanami](e2011.html)), including the typical timing for best viewing in the average year and japan-guide.com ratings. See also our nationwide list of [famous cherry blossom spots](e2011_where.html).

* 

  Tateyama Park•

  Late March to mid April

  20 minutes by bus from Nagasaki Station  
  Admission: free

  Tateyama Park is located on one of the hills surrounding [Nagasaki City](e2162.html). About 700 cherry trees are planted on terraces all along the hillside, with views over the city. It is a popular place for holding picnics and parties under the trees. The park is lit up in the evenings.
* 

  Sakura no Sato•

  Late March to mid April

  Almost one hour by bus north of Nagasaki Station  
  Admission: free

  Sakura no Sato is a community park in a residential district, still technically located within the city limits of Nagasaki, but almost a one hour bus ride outside of the downtown area. Besides some facilities for the community, like tennis courts and playgrounds for children, the park contains several thousands of cherry trees.
* 

  [Nagasaki Peace Park](e4400.html)

  Late March to mid April

  Peace Park tram stop  
  Admission: free

  [Nagasaki Peace Park](e4400.html) has around 200 cherry trees in the Hypocenter Park and several more trees located toward the Atomic Bomb Museum.
