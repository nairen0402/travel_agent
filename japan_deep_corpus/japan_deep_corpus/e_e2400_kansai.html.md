* 

  [Day 1 - Osaka](e2157.html)

  Spend the day in [Osaka](e2057.html), Japan's second most important metropolis.
* 

  [Day 2 - Day trip to Himeji](e3500.html)

  Go on a day trip to [Himeji](e3500.html) to see [Himeji Castle](e3501.html) and [Mount Shosha](e3503.html).
* 

  [Day 3 - Osaka to Kinosaki](e3525.html)

  Take a [limited express](e2016.html) train from [Osaka](e2157.html) to [Kinosaki](e3525.html). Explore the quaint [hot spring](e2292.html) town and stay overnight at one of the [ryokan](e2029.html) there.
* 

  [Day 4 - Kinosaki to Kyoto via Amanohashidate](e3990.html)

  From Kinosaki, take trains to [Amanohashidate](e3990.html), one of Japan's [three most scenic views](e2250.html), before heading to [Kyoto](e2158.html). Alternatively, visit rural [Miyama](e3985.html) to see traditional thatched roof farmhouses along the way to Kyoto.
* 

  [Day 5 and 6 - Kyoto](e2158.html)

  Spend two full days in [Kyoto](e2158.html) exploring the many treasures the old capital has to offer. Consider our [suggested Kyoto itineraries](e3950.html).
* 

  [Day 7 - Kyoto to Nara](e2165.html)

  Travel from [Kyoto](e2158.html) to [Nara](e2165.html), Japan's first permanent capital, and overnight there.
* 

  [Day 8 - Nara to Ise](e4301.html)

  Leave Nara after breakfast to go to Ise (about two hours by train), the site of Japan's most sacred [Shinto](e2056.html) shrines, the [Ise Shrines](e4300.html). Consider stopping at [Iga Ueno](e4350.html) along the way to learn about [ninja](e2295.html).
* 

  [Day 9 - Ise to Kumano](e4950.html)

  Take a train into the [Kumano](e4950.html) area at the southern end of the Kii Peninsula.
* 

  [Day 10 - Kumano](e4950.html)

  Spend a full day exploring [Kumano](e4950.html) with its ancient shrines and pilgrimage trails.
* 

  [Day 11 - Kumano to Mount Koya](e4900.html)

  Get up early and head to [Koyasan](e4900.html), the center of [Shingon Buddhism](e2055.html), and one of the best places to experience an overnight stay at a [temple lodging](e4905.html).
* 

  [Day 12 - Mount Koya to Osaka](e2157.html)

  Head back to [Osaka](e2157.html).

The above itinerary is somewhat fast-paced. Tourists preferring a slow pace of travel should consider spending more time at some of the destinations along the way.
