Garden design is an important [art](e639.html) form which has been refined for over 1000 years. Gardens have evolved into a [variety of styles](e2099_types.html) with different purposes, including strolling gardens for the recreation of [feudal](e2128.html) lords and dry stone gardens for religious use by [monks](e2055.html). Outstanding gardens can be found across Japan, with particularly many in the former capital of [Kyoto](e2158.html).

Below are Japan's best gardens according to japan-guide.com's editors and users:

* 

  1

  [Kanazawa](e2167.html)

  [Kenrokuen](e4200.html)

  Built in [Kanazawa](e2167.html) for the enjoyment of the former local lords, [Kenrokuen](e4200.html) feels like a collection of multiple excellent gardens, which include a variety of ponds, streams, hills, groves, flower gardens, pavilions and tea houses.
* 

  2

  [Near Matsue](e5800.html)

  [Adachi Museum of Art](e5806.html)

  Meticulously maintained, the relatively recently created garden at the [Adachi Museum of Art](e5806.html) near [Matsue](e5800.html) is almost surreal. The museum's collection of Japanese paintings is also outstanding.
* 

  3

  [Kyoto](e2158.html)

  [Katsura Imperial Villa](e3914.html)

  The strolling garden of the [Katsura Imperial Villa](e3914.html) in [Kyoto](e2158.html) is one of the first and finest of its kind. Visitors need to join a guided tour to see the garden.
* 

  4

  [Kyoto](e2158.html)

  [Kokedera](e3937.html)

  The moss-covered garden of [Kyoto](e2158.html)'s [Kokedera](e3937.html) (Moss Temple) is one of the country's most celebrated. Advance reservations by mail and participation in a sutra copying session are required to see the garden.
* 

  5

  [Takamatsu](e5400.html)

  [Ritsurin Koen](e5401.html)

  The recreational garden of the former local lords, [Ritsurin Koen](e5401.html) in [Takamatsu](e5400.html) is one of the most beautiful strolling gardens in Japan, featuring ponds, tea pavilions and walking trails.
* 

  6

  [Kyoto](e2158.html)

  [Ryoanji](e3909.html)

  [Kyoto](e2158.html)'s [Ryoanji Temple](e3909.html) is home to the most famous of all stone gardens in Japan. Try to visit on weekdays in the early morning or late afternoon to reduce the crowds.
* 

  7

  [Okayama](e5700.html)

  [Korakuen](e5701.html)

  [Korakuen](e5701.html) is another of the best strolling gardens of Japan. It stands next to [Okayama Castle](e5702.html) whose former residents would use it for the entertainment of guests and themselves.
* 

  8

  [Kyoto](e2158.html)

  [Daitokuji](e3910.html)

  [Daitokuji Temple](e3910.html) is the ultimate destination for those interested in Zen gardens. Consisting of nearly two dozen subtemples, Daitokuji offers a large number and variety of excellent gardens.
* 

  9

  [Yokohama](e2156.html)

  [Sankeien](e3205.html)

  The best garden in the [Greater Tokyo](/list/e1103.html) area, [Yokohama](e2156.html)'s [Sankeien](e3205.html) features a pond and a variety of historic buildings moved there from across Japan.
* 

  10

  [Uji](e3975.html)

  [Byodoin](e3923.html)

  [Byodoin](e3923.html) is the best surviving example of a [Pure Land Garden](e2099_types.html). Its celebrated Phoenix Hall appears on the 10 yen coin. A unique museum with temple treasures stands nearby.
