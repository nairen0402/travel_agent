duration

6-8 hours

budget

Around 1500 yen

pace

Medium pace

The following is a full-day [model course](e2400.html) for average-paced travelers that covers the major attractions in the town center of [Takayama](e5900.html) on foot over about seven kilometers.

Start

Start at **Takayama Station**

**Walk**

15 minutes

Takayama Jinya

Historically important building
40 minutes

A beautiful traditional Japanese style building, the [Takayama Jinya](e5902.html) used to serve as Takayama's government office during the [Edo Period](e2128.html), when the city stood under direct control of the shogun due to its valuable timber resources.

**Walk**

5 minutes

Sanmachi Suji

Central street of Takayama's old town
30-90 minutes

The epicenter of Takayama's [old town](e5903.html), this street boasts numerous beautifully preserved houses dating from the [Edo Period](e2128.html). The buildings today contain a range of attractions including cafes, shops and [sake](e2037_sake.html) breweries.

**Walk**

10 minutes

Kusakabe Heritage House

Heritage House
30 minutes

The [Kusakabe Heritage House](e5903.html) (Kusakabe Mingeikan) is one of Takayama's oldest merchant homes open to the public. Its former residents, the Kusakabe family, used to be successful money lenders.

**Walk**

5 minutes

Hachiman Shrine

Shrine that hosts Takayama's autumn festival
30 minutes

Sakurayama Hachiman Shrine (Sakurayama Hachimangu) stands in the northeastern area of Takayama's old town. The [shrine](e2059.html)'s festival, the [Takayama Autumn Festival](e5909.html), is celebrated annually on October 9-10.

**Walk**

15 minutes

Higashiyama Walking Course

Hiking trail in the outskirts of town
120 minutes

A 3.5 kilometer walk through the hills on the outskirts of Takayama's town center, this route passes various shrines and temples and the ruins of the city's former castle.

Finish

* 

  [Matsuri Yatai Kaikan](e5905.html)

  Located just next to Hachiman Shrine, the [Takayama Yatai Kaikan](e5905.html) (festival float exhibition hall) exhibits four of the [autumn festival](e5909.html)'s eleven festival floats. English audio guides are available.
* 

  [Karakuri Museum](e5903.html#shishi)

  Also located at Hachiman Shrine, the Karakuri Museum (mechanical doll museum) displays mechanical dolls (karakuri ningyo) which get mounted onto [festival floats](e5909.html). The museum also shows over 200 shishimai (lion dance) masks from around Japan.
