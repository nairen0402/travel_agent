Japan has many, beautifully arranged open air museums, displaying historic buildings from various [regions of Japan](e623a.html) and historical periods. Some of the best are listed below:

* 

  [Sapporo](e2163.html)

  [Historic Village of Hokkaido](e5303.html)

  About 60 buildings

  This open air museum's theme is the development of Japan's northernmost island of [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html), which took place on a large scale during the [Meiji Period](e2130.html) (1868 to 1912).
* 

  [Abashiri](e6865.html)

  [Abashiri Prison Museum](e6867.html)

  About 12 buildings

  This museum exhibits the former buildings of the Abashiri Prison and illustrates the everyday life of the prisoners.
* 

  [Kitakami](e5077.html)

  [Michinoku Folklore Village](e5077.html)

  About 30 buildings

  This open air museum exhibits various farmhouses and other buildings from different historical periods from the [Tohoku Region](/list/e1102.html).
* 

  [Narita](e6400.html)

  [Boso no Mura](e6403.html)

  About 30 buildings

  Nor far from [Narita Airport](e2027.html), Boso no Mura ("Boso Village") displays traditional local lifestyle and crafts of the area around [Chiba Prefecture](/list/e1212.html). It reproduces a typical local village of the [Edo Period](e2128.html), complete with merchant houses, workshops and farms.
* 

  [Tokyo](e2164.html)

  [Edo-Tokyo Open Air Architectural Museum](e3032.html)

  About 20 buildings

  The "outdoor section" of the Edo-Tokyo Museum, this open air museum features various historical buildings of the [Tokyo](e2164.html) area, mainly from the [Meiji Period](e2130.html) (1868-1912).
* 

  [Kawasaki](e3250.html)

  [Nihon Minkaen](e3253.html)

  About 25 buildings

  This open air museum in the suburbs of [Tokyo](e2164.html) exhibits preserved various buildings from the [Edo Period](e2128.html) which were relocated to [Kawasaki](e3250.html) from all over Japan, including farmhouses, [samurai](e2127.html) houses, merchant houses and more.
* 

  [Inuyama](e3350.html)

  [Meiji Mura](e3353.html)

  About 60 buildings

  One of Japan's largest and best open air museums, Meiji Mura ("Meiji Village") exhibits buildings from across Japan, which were built during the [Meiji Period](e2130.html) (1868-1912).
* 

  [Takayama](e5900.html)

  [Hida Folk Village](e5901.html)

  About 25 buildings

  The outstanding Hida Folk Village exhibits various buildings, including a few gassho-zukuri houses, from Hida, the mountainous region around [Takayama](e5900.html).
* 

  [Shirakawa-go](e5950.html)

  [Gassho-zukuri Minkaen](e5951.html)

  About 20 buildings

  This museum exhibits and conserves numerous gasshozukuri farmhouses and other structures, which were relocated here from surrounding villages.
* 

  [Takamatsu](e5400.html)

  [Shikoku Mura](e5402.html)

  About 30 buildings

  The Shikoku Mura ("Shikoku Village") displays buildings and structures from various [historical periods](e2126.html) and from across the island of [Shikoku](/list/e1107.html).
* 

  [Nagasaki](e2162.html)

  [Glover Garden](e4406.html)

  About 10 buildings

  This garden with good views of [Nagasaki](e2162.html), exhibits the former homes of Western residents and other Western style buildings, dating from the second half of the [19th century](e2128.html).
