JAL and ANA operate multiple [flights](e2365.html) per day between [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s [Haneda Airport](e2430.html) and Akita Airport. Flight duration is about 70 minutes. The regular one way fare is around 35,000 yen, however, [discount tickets](e2364.html) are usually available for around 12,000 to 19,000 yen. Buses connect Akita Airport with Akita Station in the city center (40 minutes, 1200 yen one way).

Direct Komachi trains on the [JR Akita Shinkansen](e2018_tohoku.html) travel between [Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Akita](e3625.html). The one way trip takes around four hours, costs about 18,000 yen and all seats require seat reservations. There are hourly departures, and the trip is fully covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html), [JR East Tohoku Area Pass](e2361_tohoku.html) and [JR East South Hokkaido Pass](e2361_southhokkaido.html).

Akita Chuo Kotsu, Odakyu Bus and JR Bus Tohoku operate [overnight buses](e2366.html) between Tokyo and Akita. The one way journey takes about eight hours and costs between 6,900 and 9,900 yen depending on the day of travel and the timing of the purchase. Online reservations can be made through [Willer](https://willerexpress.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/akita/all/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184) and [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en/AreaSearch2/3130000/2052019/?afcd=MDI=).

Akita City is relatively small compared to other [prefectural](/list/e1002.html) capitals, and the city is best explored on foot. The major attractions are located to the west of the station, with the [Akita Museum of Art](e3628.html) about a ten minute walk away, and Chuo Dori, the main street where the Kanto Festival is held, an additional five minutes walk. Akita Station is the city's transportation hub where all the regional and interregional [train](e2019.html) and [bus](e2015.html) lines meet, including the [Akita Shinkansen](e2018.html) and airport buses to Akita Airport (40 minutes, 1200 yen one way).
