[Fukushima](e7725.html) and [Tokyo](e2164.html) are connected by frequent Yamabiko and Tsubasa trains along the [JR Tohoku Shinkansen](e2018_tohoku.html). The one way trip takes around 90 minutes and costs about 9000 yen. There are about two departures per hour. The entire journey is covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html), [JR East Tohoku Area Pass](e2361_tohoku.html) and [JR East South Hokkaido Pass](e2361_southhokkaido.html).

Multiple bus companies operate daytime and overnight [highway buses](e2366.html) between Tokyo ([Tokyo Station](e3037.html) and [Shinjuku Station](e3011.html)) and Fukushima Station. The one way trip takes around five hours and costs between around 3000 and 6000 yen depending on the company and date of travel. Online reservations can be made through [Willer](https://willerexpress.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/fukushima/fukushima/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184) and [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en/CourseSearch/10200050004,11300310002?afcd=MDI=&RosenNm=fukushima-tokyo).

Above fees and schedules are subject to change. Be sure to check current yen [exchange rates](e2196.html).

[Buses](e2015.html) provide transportation within Fukushima City; however the attractions outside of the city center, such as [Takayu Onsen](e7729.html) and the [Azuma Mountains](e7754.html), are not well served by public transportation and are better accessed by [rental car](e2024.html). Rental car outlets can be found around Fukushima Station. Note that the [Bandai-Azuma Skyline](e7754.html), a former toll road, is now free to use.
