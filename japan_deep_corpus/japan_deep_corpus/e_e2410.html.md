This page is meant to give you a rough idea of the cost of individual travel in Japan by introducing some sample budgets. Note that [accommodation](e2025.html) rates can increase during [peak seasons](e2273.html) in popular destinations which is not reflected by the numbers below. View also our pages about [budget travel](e2028.html), [package tours](e2275.html) and [suggested itineraries](e2400.html) to see the sample budgets applied to specific itineraries.

### Sample daily budgets

The numbers below include the costs for accommodation, meals and sightseeing, but do not include the cost for transportation. [Click here](e2196.html) for the current yen exchange rates.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
|  | **Single Traveler** | **Two Travelers** |
| **Low Budget** | 5,000 - 13,000 yen | 9,000 - 19,000 yen |
| **Medium Budget** | 13,000 - 28,000 yen | 19,000 - 46,000 yen |
| **High Budget** | over 28,000 yen | over 46,000 yen |

### Accommodation

Below are typical rates for [accommodation](e2025.html) outside the [peak seasons](e2273.html). Rates can increase during the peak seasons. Check our [accommodation page](e2025.html) for more information on different accommodation types and corresponding price ranges.

**Low budget:**
  
3,000 - 10,000 yen per single room and night
  
6,000 - 12,000 yen per double room and night
  
Dormitories and [hostels](e2030.html), found in most cities of Japan, typically charge below 5000 yen per night and person. Furthermore, booking services like [Hostelworld](https://hostelworld.prf.hn/click/camref:1011l4d95/destination:https://www.hostelworld.com/st/hostels/asia/japan/) offer great deals.

**Medium budget:**
  
10,000 - 20,000 yen per single room and night
  
12,000 - 30,000 yen per double room and night
  
At this level, you will find rooms in [business hotels](e2025_business_hotels.html), Western-style hotels and simple [ryokan](e2029.html).

**High budget:**
  
over 20,000 yen per single room and night
  
over 30,000 yen per double room and night
  
Starting around 20,000 yen per person, you can get rooms in better hotels. A stay at a [ryokan](e2029.html) with two meals included typically costs between 15,000 and 40,000 yen per person and night. Room rates at 5-star hotels start around 80,000 yen per night.

### Breakfast

**Low budget:**
  
500 yen per day
  
At this level, you will have to live from [convenience store](e2071.html) food (bread, rice balls, etc.) and fast food [restaurants](e2036.html) like McDonald's or Mister Donut which offer inexpensive breakfasts.

**Medium budget:**
  
500 - 1000 yen per day
  
Many coffee shops and some [restaurants](e2036.html) in shopping areas and around train stations offer breakfast sets for around 500 to 1000 yen.

**High budget:**
  
above 1000 yen per day
  
Hotel breakfasts and breakfast buffets will usually cost you more than 1000 yen. Breakfast buffets in first-class hotels typically cost at least 3000 yen.

### Lunch

**Low budget:**
  
500 - 800 yen per day
  
Inexpensive lunch boxes are available in [convenience stores](e2071.html) and stands in railway stations and business districts. Various fast food [restaurants](e2036.html), specializing in noodles, curry, [domburi](e2342.html) or hamburger, also offer relatively filling meals for 800 yen or less.

**Medium budget:**
  
800 - 1500 yen per day
  
At this level you will have an even larger range of inexpensive [restaurants](e2036.html), such as the above-mentioned fast food places, plus restaurants offering lunch sets for around 1000 yen.

**High budget:**
  
above 1500 yen per day
  
A lunch at better [restaurants](e2036.html) costs typically between 1500 and 3000 yen.

### Dinner

**Low budget:**
  
500 - 1000 yen per day
  
[Convenience stores](e2071.html) sell various, inexpensive ready-to-eat meals. Other options are again cheap fast food [restaurants](e2036.html).

**Medium budget:**
  
1000 - 2500 yen per day
  
Conventional [restaurants](e2036.html) are generally more expensive in the evening than during lunch time, as there are no lunch specials. At this level, you will be able to enjoy a nice dinner at a wide range of fast food and conventional restaurants, including the restaurants found in [department stores](e2072.html) where meals typically cost between 1000 and 2500 yen, not including alcoholic drinks.

**High budget:**
  
above 2500 yen per day
  
With 3000 yen per person you will be able to have a good dinner at a wide range of [restaurants](e2036.html). Calculate 5000 yen upwards per person for a dinner at upper class restaurants specializing in [sushi](e2038.html), French cuisine, sukiyaki, kaiseki ryori, steaks, etc.

### Sightseeing

**Low budget:**
  
0 - 1000 yen per day
  
Japan offers many free attractions. Most [shrines](e2059.html) and some [temples](e2058.html) do not charge fees.

**Medium budget:**
  
1000 - 3000 yen per day
  
Admission to famous [temples](e2058.html) costs between 500 and 1000 yen. Most museums and [castles](e2296.html) charge about 500-1500 yen per person.

**High budget:**
  
above 3000 yen per day
  
Some museums and attractions (usually outstanding ones or tourist traps) charge between 1,500 and 3,000 yen per person. Admission to large [theme parks](e2067.html) typically cost around 5,000 to 10,000 yen per day.

### Transportation

**Low budget:**
  
[Highway buses](e2366.html) are among the cheapest ways of traveling in Japan.

**All budgets:**
  
Travelers of all budgets should consider purchasing a [rail pass](e2357.html). Over long distances, [domestic flights](e2365.html) are often more economical, if you take advantage of the various [discount offers](e2364.html).
