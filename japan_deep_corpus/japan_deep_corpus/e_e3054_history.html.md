### History

* 

  [Tokyo National Museum••](e3054_tokyo.html)

  5 minute walk from Ueno Station in [Ueno Park](e3019.html)  
  Hours: 9:30 to 17:00 (until 19:00 on Fridays and Saturdays)  
  Closed: Mondays (or following day if Monday is a [holiday](e2062.html)), [New Year](e2276.html) holidays  
  Admission: 1000 yen

  The oldest and largest museum in Japan, the [Tokyo National Museum](e3054_tokyo.html) is made up of multiple buildings, each like a separate museum in itself. They house the largest collection of national treasures and important cultural items in the country.
* 

  [Edo-Tokyo Museum••](e3070.html)

  Closed for renovations through March 30, 2026

  The excellent [Edo-Tokyo Museum](e3070.html) displays models and life-sized recreations of structures and events to show the daily life of Tokyo during the [Edo](e2128.html), [Meiji](e2130.html) and early [Showa](e2129.html) periods.
* 

  [Edo-Tokyo Open Air Museum](e3032.html)

  10 minute bus ride from Musashi-Koganei Station  
  Hours: 9:30 to 17:30 (until 16:30 from Oct to Mar)  
  Closed: Mondays (or following day if Monday is a [holiday](e2062.html)), [New Year](e2276.html) holidays  
  Admission: 400 yen

  A branch of the above Edo-Tokyo Museum, the [Edo-Tokyo Open Air Museum](e3032.html) exhibits a number of relocated and reconstructed historic buildings and structures laid out like a historic town.
* 

  [Yushukan](e2321.html)

  On the grounds of [Yasukuni Shrine](e2321.html)  
  Hours: 9:00 to 16:30 (entry until 16:00)  
  Closed: A few irregular closure days in late June and late December  
  Admission: 1000 yen

  A large museum on the grounds of [Yasukuni Shrine](e2321.html) that commemorates Japan's wars from a conservative Japanese perspective with detailed information in English.
* 

  Shitamachi Museum

  5 minute walk from [Ueno Station](e3019.html)  
  Hours: 9:30 to 16:30 (entry until 16:00)  
  Closed: Mondays (or following day if Monday is a [holiday](e2062.html)), Dec 29 to Jan 3  
  Admission: 300 yen

  Shitamachi is the name of the artisan and merchant part of old Tokyo. This nostalgic museum has exhibits and reconstructions that show what life was like in Tokyo from the late [Meiji](e2130.html) to early [Showa Periods](e2129.html).
* 

  The Japanese Sword Museum

  5 minute walk north of [Ryogoku Station](e3022.html)  
  Hours: 9:30 to 17:00 (entry until 16:30)  
  Closed: Mondays (or next day if Mon is a [holiday](e2062.html)), [New Year](e2276.html), between exhibitions  
  Admission: 1000 yen

  The Japanese Sword Museum exhibits a large number of sword blades and related items, including historic and modern items. Exhibitions are changed multiple times per year. Formerly located in [Shinjuku](e3011.html), the museum was moved to a modern building in [Ryogoku](e3022.html) in 2018.

### Science

* 

  National Museum of Emerging Science and Innovation••

  5 min walk from Tokyo International Cruise Terminal Station ([Odaiba](e3008.html))  
  Hours: 10:00 to 17:00 (entry until 16:30)  
  Closed: Tuesdays (except [national holidays](e2062.html)) and Dec 28 to Jan 1  
  Admission: 630 yen

  Also known as Miraikan, this extremely interactive and bilingual museum has excellent exhibits about environmental issues, robots, information technology, biology and space exploration.
* 

  National Science Museum•

  5 min walk from Ueno Station in [Ueno Park](e3019.html)  
  Hours: 9:00 to 17:00 (until 20:00 on Fridays and Saturdays)  
  Closed: Mondays (or following day if Mon is a [holiday](e2062.html)), Dec 28 to Jan 1  
  Admission: 630 yen

  This museum covers both science and natural history with hands-on physics and robotics experiments, an impressive collection of mounted animals and a 360 degree virtual theater.
