Take the [JR Tokaido/Sanyo Shinkansen](e2018.html) from [Tokyo](e2164.html) to [Okayama](e5700.html) (3.5 hours) and transfer to a JR Shiokaze [limited express](e2016.html) train for [Matsuyama](e5500.html) (2.5 hours). The entire one way trip takes around 6.5 hours and costs about 22,000 yen. The [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html) fully covers the entire trip if [Hikari or Sakura](e2018.html) trains are used between Tokyo and Okayama, but a supplement fee is required on the faster [Nozomi and Mizuho](e2018.html) trains.

Several overnight [highway buses](e2366.html) are operated daily between [Tokyo](e2164.html) and [Matsuyama](e5500.html). The regular one way fare is around 12,000 yen. This route is also served by discount buses companies that offer lower fares. Online reservations can be made through [Willer](https://willer-travel.com/en/bus_search/tokyo/all/ehime/matsuyama/?utm_source=web&utm_medium=ad&utm_campaign=af01&aid=184) and [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en/CourseSearch/11300630005?afcd=MDI=&RosenNm=tokyo-matsuyama).

Matsuyama has two main train stations: **JR Matsuyama Station** to the west of the city center and **Matsuyama-shi Station** (sometimes referred to as Matsuyama City Station), the main station of the local Iyotetsu Railway company in the south of downtown. [Matsuyama Castle](e5501.html) defines the city center, while [Dogo Onsen](e5502.html) is located on the northeastern outskirts of the city.

The most convenient way to travel around the city is by **tram**. Iyotetsu operates five tram lines across the city, connecting JR Matsuyama Station in the west to Matsuyama-shi Station in the south and [Dogo Onsen](e5502.html) area to the northeast. A tram ride costs 210 yen regardless of distance traveled if paying with an [IC card](e2359_003.html) or 230 yen if paying by cash. To use the tram, board through the rear door just like on [local buses](e2015.html) and pay the fare when alighting.

Tram in Matsuyama

Iyotetsu also operates an extensive [bus](e2015.html) network in [Matsuyama](e5500.html). For most tourists, the trams provide adequate access to all sightseeing areas. Three buses however, may be of interest: the first operates between Dogo Onsen and [Ishiteji Temple](e5503.html) (5 minutes, 230-250 yen). Two other buses shuttle passengers to Matsuyama Airport and Matsuyama Port (see below). On all buses, the use of an [IC card](e2359_003.html) over cash will result in a 20 yen fare reduction.

**Matsuyama Airport** lies on the city's southwestern boundary. Airport buses serve JR Matsuyama Station (980-1000 yen, 15 minutes), Matsuyama-shi Station (980-1000 yen, 20 minutes) and Dogo Onsen (1180-1200 yen, 35 minutes). Besides domestic destinations, Matsuyama Airport also has a few international flights to nearby Asian destinations.

**Matsuyama Port** (Matsuyama Sightseeing Port) is located northwest of the city center. From Matsuyama-shi Station, take the Iyotetsu Takahama Line to Takahama Station (20 minutes, 470-490 yen), from where the port is a short bus ride (2 minutes, 230-250 yen) or a 10-15 minute walk.
