* 

  [Day 1 - Hakodate](e5350.html)

  Start your day in [Hakodate](e5350.html) exploring the [morning market](e5353.html) as well as Japan's first western style fortress, [Fort Goryokaku](e5352.html). Head for [Mount Hakodate](e5354.html) towards sunset and enjoy the celebrated night view over the city.
* 

  [Day 2 - Hakodate to Lake Toya](e6725.html)

  Travel by train from Hakodate to [Lake Toya](e6725.html). Take the [Usuzan Ropeway](e6726.html) to get panoramic views of Lake Toya and [Showa Shinzan](e6728.html) or follow the walking trails around the [mountain's newer craters](e6727.html) to see ruins caused by the eruption in 2000. Overnight in Toyako Onsen.
* 

  [Day 3 - Lake Toya to Noboribetsu](e6750.html)

  Not far from [Lake Toya](e6725.html) is [Noboribetsu](e6750.html), Hokkaido's most celebrated [hot spring resort](e2292_where.html). Enjoy the high quality waters and explore the water's source, the [hell valley](e6751.html). Overnight at one of the town's onsen ryokan.
* 

  [Day 4 and 5 - Sapporo](e2163.html)

  Proceed to [Sapporo](e2163.html), the capital city of [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html), and spend the next three nights there. Attractions in Sapporo include [Moerenuma Park](e5313.html), [Mount Moiwa](e5309.html) and [Susukino](e5305.html) among others.
* 

  [Day 6 - Day trip to Otaru](e6700.html)

  Make a side trip to [Otaru](e6700.html), about 40 minutes from [Sapporo](e2163.html) by train. Check out the beautifully preserved [canal area](e6701.html) and [merchant street](e6704.html).
* 

  [Day 7 - Sapporo to Furano](e6825.html)

  Travel from Sapporo to [Furano](e6825.html), known for its [flower fields](e6826.html). Overnight in Furano.
* 

  [Day 8 - Furano to Asahidake Onsen via Biei](e6828.html)

  Drop by [Biei](e6828.html), a town surrounded by picturesque landscape and flower fields on your way to [Asahidake Onsen](e6776.html). Stay in Asahidake Onsen for the next two nights.
* 

  [Day 9 - Daisetsuzan National Park](e6775.html)

  From Asahidake Onsen, explore [Daisetsuzan National Park](e6775.html), Japan's largest [national park](e2325.html) and a paradise for hikers and outdoor lovers.
* 

  [Day 10 - Asahidake Onsen to Asahikawa](e6890.html)

  Proceed to [Asahikawa](/e/e6890.html), [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html)'s second largest city and best best known for its [zoo](/e/e6892.html) and [ramen](/e/e6893.html). Stay one night in Asahikawa.
* 

  [Day 11 - Asahikawa to Sounkyo](e6777.html)

  Travel from Asahikawa to [Sounkyo Onsen](/e/e6777.html), a [hot spring](/e/e2292.html) in [Daisetsuzan National Park](/e/e6775.html). The town is also a good base for walks and hikes further into the national park with ropeway access to [Mount Kurodake](/e/e6783.html).
* 

  [Day 12 and 14 - Shiretoko National Park](e6850.html)

  Travel from Sounkyo to [Shiretoko National Park](/e/e6850.html), another of Japan's most beautiful and unspoiled [national parks](/e/e2325.html). Enjoy exploring the national park and spend two nights on the peninsula.
* 

  [Day 15 - Shiretoko to Akan](e6800.html)

  Proceed southwards from Shiretoko to [Kawayu Onsen](/e/e6806.html) in [Akan National Park](/e/e6800.html). The national park is known for its three beautiful lakes: [Lake Akan](/e/e6801.html), [Lake Mashu](/e/e6802.html) and [Lake Kussharo](/e/e6803.html).
* 

  [Day 16 - Akan to Kushiro](e6792.html)

  Stop by the [Kushiro Marshland](/e/e6792.html) on the way to [Kushiro](e6790.html). The park supports the only known population of endangered Japanese cranes. Kushiro Airport provides flights to Sapporo, Tokyo and a few other destinations.

The above itinerary is somewhat fast-paced. Tourists preferring a slow pace of travel should consider spending more time at some of the destinations along the way.
