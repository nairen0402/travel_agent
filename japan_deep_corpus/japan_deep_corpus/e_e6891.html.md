JAL and Air Do (co-shared with ANA) operate multiple [flights](e2365.html) per day between [Tokyo](e2164.html)'s [Haneda Airport](e2430.html) and Asahikawa Airport, while Jetstar Japan connects to Tokyo's [Narita Airport](e2027.html). The regular one way fare by JAL is around 45,000 yen, however, [discount tickets](e2364.html) are typically available for between 12,000 and 21,000 yen. The fares by Air Do are typically around 20 percent lower than those by JAL, while fares by Jestar Japan start from around 6000 yen. [Buses](e2015.html) connect Asahikawa Airport with central [Asahikawa](e6890.html) (35 minutes, 750 yen, departures timed to flights).

The journey from [Tokyo](e2164.html) to [Asahikawa](e6890.html) by [trains](e2019.html) involves transfers at Shin-Hakodate-Hokuto and [Sapporo](e2163.html), takes about 10 hours, costs around 33,000 yen one way and is fully covered by the [Japan Rail Pass](e2361.html).

Above fees and schedules are subject to change. Be sure to check current yen [exchange rates](e2196.html).

[Asahikawa](e6890.html) is located in the center of [Hokkaido](/list/e1101.html) and well connected with the prefectural capital of [Sapporo](e2163.html) by [train](e2019.html), [bus](e2366.html) and [expressway](e2354.html). Urban public transportation is provided by a [bus](e2015.html) network, which also connects Asahikawa Station in the city center with [Asahiyama Zoo](e6892.html) in the city's eastern outskirts.

Asahikawa Airport is located 10 kilometers south of the city and provides [flights](e2365.html) to [Tokyo](e2164.html) ([Haneda Airport](e2430.html)), [Osaka](e2157.html) ([Kansai](e2033.html) and [Itami](e2433.html) Airports), [Nagoya](e2155.html) and Seoul. The airport is connected with the city center by [bus](e2015.html) (35 minutes, 750 yen).

Asahikawa can serve as a base for exploring [Furano](e6825.html) and [Biei](e6828.html) to the south and [Daisetsuzan National Park](e6775.html) to the southeast.
