* 

  [Day 1 - Osaka to Okayama via Himeji and Kurashiki](e5750.html)

  Take the [shinkansen](e2018.html) from [Osaka](e2157.html) to [Okayama](e5700.html), stopping at [Himeji](e3500.html) along the way. From Okayama, visit nearby [Kurashiki](e5750.html) with its picturesque [canal area](e5751.html) or see Okayama's [Korakuen Garden](e5701.html).
* 

  [Day 2 - Okayama to Naoshima](e5475.html)

  Take a train and ferry from Okayama via Uno Port to the modern art island of [Naoshima](e5475.html). See the art and overnight on Naoshima.
* 

  [Day 3 - Naoshima to Takamatsu via Teshima](e5400.html)

  Take a ferry from Naoshima via Teshima Island to [Takamatsu](e5400.html). Rural [Teshima Island](e5440.html) is the site of another outstanding modern art museum. In Takamatsu, enjoy the local specialty, [sanuki udon](e5408.html), for dinner.
* 

  [Day 4 - Takamatsu to Matsuyama](e5400.html)

  Explore [Takamatsu](e5400.html) with its [Ritsurin Koen](e5401.html) before taking the [limited express train](e2016.html) to [Matsuyama](e5500.html). Stay overnight at [Dogo Onsen](e5502.html).
* 

  [Day 5 - Matsuyama](e5500.html)

  Spend one day exploring [Matsuyama](e5500.html).
* 

  [Day 6 - Cycle the Shimanami Kaido](e3478.html)

  In the early morning, take a train from [Matsuyama](e5500.html) to Imabari, the start of the [Shimanami Kaido Cycling Road](e3478.html). Rent bicycles and cycle across the 70 kilometer long cycling road before returning the bicycles in [Onomichi](e3475.html). Stay overnight in Onomichi.
* 

  [Day 7 - Onomichi to Hiroshima](e2160.html)

  Spend the morning in the sleepy seaside town of [Onomichi](e3475.html) and do the [Temple Walk](e3477.html) that connects 25 temples in Onomichi. Head on to [Hiroshima](/list/e1238.html) and spend the afternoon and night there.
* 

  [Day 8 - Hiroshima to Miyajima](e3401.html)

  Take a ferry over to [Miyajima](e3401.html) and spend one night on the sacred island with its famous shrine and torii gate built over water.
* 

  [Day 9 - Miyajima to Hagi via Iwakuni](e6175.html)

  Take the ferry and local train from Miyajima to [Iwakuni](e6175.html) and explore the city's unique [Kintai-kyo Bridge](e6177.html). Carry on to [Hagi](e6150.html) on the Sea of Japan coast and overnight there.
* 

  [Day 10 - Hagi](e6150.html)

  Spend a full day exploring [Hagi](e6150.html)'s old castle town and other attractions.
* 

  [Day 11 - Hagi to Matsue via Izumo](e5804.html)

  On the way from Hagi to Matsue, visit [Izumo Taisha](e5804.html), one of Japan's most important and oldest [shrines](e2059.html). If time permits, check out [Iwami Ginzan](e5850.html), a world heritage site, as well. Overnight in [Matsue](e5800.html).
* 

  [Day 12 - Matsue to Osaka](e5800.html)

  Explore more of [Matsue](e5800.html) with its [castle](e5801.html) and pay a visit to the spectacular [Adachi Museum of Art](e5806.html) before heading back to [Osaka](e2157.html).

The above itinerary is somewhat fast-paced. Tourists preferring a slow pace of travel should consider spending more time at some of the destinations along the way.
